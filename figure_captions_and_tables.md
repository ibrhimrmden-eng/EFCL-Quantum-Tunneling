## Figure Captions

**Figure 1. Barrier profile before and after the localized EFCL correction.**
Bare barrier V_std(x) = (V₀/2)[tanh((x+a)/w) − tanh((x−a)/w)] (V₀=1, a=1, w=0.12) compared with the effective potential V_eff(x) = V_std(x) + Φ_EFCL(x) at λ₁=0.35, λ₂=0.10, t=0. Generated directly from the potential-definition functions in `logderiv_ref.py`; no fitting involved. Shows that the corrected Φ_EFCL term vanishes at large |x|, satisfying the asymptotic-flatness condition required for a well-defined scattering problem (Section 2.1).

**Figure 2. Exact transmission versus primitive WKB.**
Transmission coefficient T(E) for the bare barrier, computed both by the validated log-derivative (Riccati) solver and by the primitive WKB exponent T≈exp(−2∫κ dx), over E∈[0.05,1.7]. Generated by direct evaluation of both methods on a common energy grid (60 points). Demonstrates that WKB's error grows from a few percent in the deep-tunneling regime to an abrupt, unphysical jump to T=1 at the barrier top, in contrast to the smooth exact curve — the numerical basis for the claim that WKB is quantitatively inadequate near and above V₀ (Section 3, Table 3).

**Figure 3. Grid convergence after the localization fix.**
(a) Suppression ratio T_EFCL/T_std at E=0.9 and (b) maximum amplitude of Φ_EFCL(x), both as a function of grid size N=500–16000. Values are the validated convergence-test results reported in Section 3.5 (efcl_v2.py). Both panels flatten to constant values by N≈2000, confirming that the corrected model is grid-converged and that the original divergence (Section 2, `efcl_test.py`) has been eliminated.

**Figure 4. Scaling collapse of R(E,λ) onto λ/λ*(E).**
(a) Raw suppression curves R(E,λ)=ln(T_EFCL/T₀) for five representative energies spanning the tunneling and over-barrier regimes. (b) The same curves rescaled by each energy's own critical scale λ*(E), defined by R(E,λ*)=−1. Computed directly from the log-derivative solver for a λ grid of 10 points per energy. The convergence of all five curves onto a single approximate profile in panel (b) is the central empirical finding of this work (Section 4).

**Figure 5. Threshold invariance of the collapse.**
Ratio λ*(R)/λ*(R=−1) as a function of energy, for four threshold definitions R∈{−0.5,−1.0,−1.5,−2.0}. Computed by re-extracting λ*(E) at each threshold from the same underlying R(E,λ) curves. Deviations from unity remain within 2–3.5% across all tested thresholds and energies, indicating the collapse is not an artifact of the specific R=−1 convention (Section 3.5.1).

**Figure 6. Robustness of the collapse to barrier geometry.**
(a) λ*(E) recomputed for three values of the edge-sharpness parameter w (0.08, 0.12, 0.20) at fixed V₀=1. (b) λ*(E/V₀) recomputed for three barrier heights V₀ (0.8, 1.0, 1.2), plotted against the common relative energy E/V₀. Both panels use the same λ*-extraction procedure as Figure 4. Panel (a) shows near-total insensitivity to edge sharpness; panel (b) shows a systematic but small rescaling with barrier height (Section 3.5.4).

**Figure 7. Sensitivity to the EFCL envelope width σ.**
λ*(E) recomputed for three values of the coupling-envelope width σ (1.0, 1.2, 1.4) at fixed barrier parameters. In contrast to Figure 6, the curves show a visible, systematic separation, quantifying the larger sensitivity of λ*(E) to the spatial profile of the correction term itself relative to the barrier's own geometric parameters (Section 3.5.5).

**Figure 8. Candidate-observable correlation map.**
Root-mean-square error (RMSE) of the best single-variable linear fit of each candidate quantity to λ*(E), for twelve tested observables (raw energy, classical action S₀, turning-point separation L, average potential V̄, two composite combinations, the scattering phase shift and its transformations, the Wigner delay, and two Jost-magnitude-related quantities). Pearson correlation coefficients are annotated on each bar. Bars for the two composite quantities (λS₀, λS₀/L) report non-constancy (coefficient of variation) rather than RMSE, since these were tested via a direct constancy check (χ*=λ*S₀) rather than a linear fit (Section 3.5.3–3.5.4 of the companion results document).

**Figure 9. Systematic exclusion map.**
Qualitative summary of all eleven tested candidate explanations for λ*(E), color-coded by outcome (excluded / weak or partial / robust). Compiled directly from the quantitative results underlying Figure 8 and the barrier/threshold/envelope robustness tests (Figures 5–7). Serves as a single-panel summary of the exclusion analysis that constitutes the paper's principal negative result.

**Figure 10. Study workflow.**
Schematic summary of the investigation's logical sequence: from the original (flawed) numerical model, through error detection and correction, solver validation, discovery of the scaling collapse, and the systematic exclusion analysis. Illustrative only; contains no quantitative data.
